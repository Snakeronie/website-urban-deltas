PARANA INFRASTRUCTRE

In the Paraná Delta a great infrastructre foundation can be found to support its many residents and the movement of goods. The infrastructre is of great economic value but as a consequence the ecosystem suffers greatly. The vast amount of fisheries, forestries and other heavy industrial facilities harm the surrounding area. When urbanizing the Orinoco Delta this must be avoided. Other more sustainable solutions must be found to provide the movement of people and goods.

To find a possible solution we can also look at the Paraná Delta. Many of the richer residents use boats as a means of transport. Currently they are mostly used as a form a leisure or as a tourist attraction but the potential to replace them for the car is an option that cannot be overlooked. A few commuter boats are already in place in the Paraná Delta and they are a great succes under the locals and tourists. There are many spots in the Orinoco Delta where the river is wide enough to support a large amount of traffic.

Next to movement of people and goods there also is the housing problem. Many of the houses inside the Paraná Delta are placed right next to the rivers and canals themselves. They are protected with levees when the water rises but as mentioned before, this halts the natural process from happening. The Paraná Delta does not provide the best solution in this case. Placing buildings next to the water does go nicely with using boats as a transportation method but the levees preventing the waters to flood the soil is not an option.
