ORINOCO SOIL CONDITIONS

The Orinoco Delta is in the middle of a tropical forest area. Sediment has been desposited for thousands of years through the Orinoco River. It is still a relatively young piece of land in which the soil has not been able to properly harden yet. To this day soil is still being deposited and the borders of the delta area continue to expand ever so slowly.

In order for the soil to be transported through the river it needs to be light in weight. This means that mostly the soil is mostly built up of clay and silt. These sediment types are mostly unsuitable for building any big structures. Sand, which is a lot better for building is also transported through the river except its deposited near the banks of the river.

The type of soil found in the Orinoco Delta is typically used for agricultural purposes. However, this is not the goal The Urban Delta Project seeks to achieve. If structures meant for living and commercial ends are to be built they need to be light in relative weight as to not sink into the soil, strurdy as to not be washed away by the flooding water and built on pillars for the water to not reach the ground floor when flooding. Levees or similar watermanagement structures are not an option for area as the flooding is an integral part of the ecological process in the Orinoco Delta region.
