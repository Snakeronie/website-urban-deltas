PARANA

Next to the capital city of Buenos Aires in Argentina the Paraná Delta can be found. The flood plains of the delta creates a lush environment for farming and other agricultural activities. The Paraná delta is fairly dense in population size, with an area of approximately 15.000km2 and 23 million inhabitants the delta can cause major damage both economically and ecological if not adequately managed.

Economic exploitation is not something new for the area. It is to be estimated that this area contributes up to 53% of the annual GDP for Argentina. This has the conseqquence that many of the economically important assets in the region recieve all of the water protection they need and the locals or small farmers in the area are left to suffer when a flood occurs. Floods are not uncommon for the area and regularly destroy homes or farms that did not recieve adequate protection.

The ecological damage of these exploitations is immense. The river is often contaminated and filled with disease. Parasites are found within peoples bowels. The introduction of foreign species also caused the local flora and fauna to fade away. Many of the once native creatures in the area are now endangered.

This shows a clear difference between the good and bad that has been done in the region. Argentina has been able to build strong and luxurious communites despite the unsuitable soil. Using boats as transportation and using the many rivers and canals as infrastructre has been a great succes to those who can afford it and those who cannot afford it are left to suffer. This must be avoided when urbanizing the Orinoco Delta.
