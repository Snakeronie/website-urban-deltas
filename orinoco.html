ORINOCO

On the South American continent, one of the longest rivers can be found in Venezuela. The Orinoco starts one of its many streams from high in the Colombian mountains ranges and streams all the way to the Atlantic Ocean. Along its path, many town and villages have developed and prospered. Close to the mouth of the river a large delta spans along part of the northern Venezuelan coast.

In many cases all over the world large cities prosper alongside a delta system, however the Orinoco is different. The Orinoco lies in the Delta Amacuro State, which is one of the 23 states in Venezuela. In this state no city larger than 100,000 inhabitants can be found. Purely based on location and historical viewpoints, the Orinoco Delta should be a large urbanized area. Historically, this piece of land is one of the closest to the European mainland and the Caribbean islands. Many of the early explorers including Columbus have sailed past the delta. The delta was one of the gateways to the new world since the river would also go far inland of what is nowadays Colombia and Venezuela.

The area has access to fresh water along the river and later the area was found to be rich in natural resources such as oil and gold. Yet the delta area of the Orinoco only has a population density of 1,4 people per km2, most of which are indigenous tribes.
