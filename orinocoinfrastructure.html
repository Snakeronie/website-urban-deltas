ORINOCO INFRASTRUCTRE

Currently there is little to no infrastructre in place in the Orinoco Delta. The town of Tucupita is only reachable with by one main road connecting it to the rest of the country. To create more roads however, would be a logistical nightmare. The continuous maintainance would be money drain. As mentioned in the infrastructre section of the Parana Delta boats would seem more feasible. Using the natural pathway the rivers have created would be a cheaper and more ecological option.

In terms of housing we should look back in time. The indegenous Warao tribes local to the Orinoco Delta have lived in the area for centuries and adapted perfectly to the wet conditions. Their housing is built on tall pillars which protect their possesions from the rising water during the raining season. This allows nature to do its work and the soil and surrounding vegetation to stay hydrated whilst the people can keep their feet dry. This also makes the generally soggy and thus unsuitable building terrain habitable for common folk.

Some space will need to be cleared out when building along the Orinoco Delta. Any trees that are cut down in the process can be used as building materials for newky placed structures. The Urban Delta Project will rely on these sorts of renewable and locally produced resources to urbanize the region. On average over 37.000 m3/s flows through the area. Hyrdoelectric dams can be put in place for as an energy resource. A lot of untapped potential lies in the Orinoco Delta ready to be used.
