PARANA SOIL CONDITIONS

The soil conditions of the Paraná delta are fairly similar to that of any other floodplain. The soil has a soggy and wet by nature and not very suitable for large scale urbanization. It is rather ironic that the Paraná delta is quite dense in population and fairly urbanized. Large parts of the floodplains are used for agricultural purposes to great effect. The downside of economic value is the disruption of the ecological processes. The flooding water is supposed to be soaked up by the plains to keep the soil hydrated. This proces is prevented because the water is unable to seep into the ground. This causes the floods to spread further than nominal levels.

To prevent this for happening the wealthier population has put levees in place. This does prevent the water from reaching their property but it also shoves the problem towards the less wealthy who do not have any water management systems in place. This causes a lot of polarization within the local communities all because of the disruption of a natural process.

These problems are easily solveable by allowing the water to soak into the ground. This will be taken into account when urbanizing the Orinoco Delta.
